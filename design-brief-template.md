# Design Brief

**Project** · Hero Section — SaaS Landing Page  
**Date** · 21 May 2026  
**Owner** · [Name, team]

---

## Goal
> Hero section for a SaaS landing page. Communicate the core value proposition immediately and drive trial sign-ups.

## Audience
> Marketing managers, 28–40, working in remote-first companies of 10–50 people. Busy and skeptical of hype — they've seen every SaaS promise before.

## Key Message
> "Ship campaigns faster without losing brand consistency."

## Tone of Voice
- [x] Bold / Direct
- [ ] Friendly / Approachable
- [x] Premium / Refined
- [ ] Playful / Energetic
- [ ] Other: ___

> Confident and clean — no jargon. Think Linear, not Mailchimp.

## Brand Constraints
| | |
|---|---|
| **Colors** | Primary: `#1A1A2E` (Navy) · Secondary: `#FFFFFF` (White) · Accent: One warm accent max — TBD |
| **Fonts** | Heading: Inter (or similar geometric sans-serif) · Body: Inter |
| **Style rules** | White backgrounds, high contrast, minimal decoration. No gradients unless very subtle. |

## Format & Platform
> Desktop landing page, 1440px wide. Hero section only.

## Avoid
> Stock photo faces, rocket ship clichés, anything that looks like a generic SaaS template. No buzzword-heavy subheadlines.

## Success Criteria
> Clear headline visible above the fold, one prominent CTA, overall feel is premium not corporate. A skeptical marketing manager should pause rather than scroll past.

---

## Claude Instructions
> Specific guidance for layout, components, or code output.

- **Layout preference** · Card-based layout, generous whitespace, strong visual hierarchy
- **Component style** · Tailwind utility classes, clean semantic HTML
- **Naming conventions** · kebab-case for classes, PascalCase for components
- **Responsiveness** · Desktop-first at 1440px; mobile breakpoint at 768px
- **Other** · CTA button must be labelled "Start free trial". No inline styles.
